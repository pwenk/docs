## Why These Nutrients Matter

Your thyroid needs specific nutrients to function optimally:

**Selenium:** Required for T4 → T3 conversion (active thyroid hormone)
**Iodine:** Building block of thyroid hormones
**Zinc:** Supports thyroid hormone production and receptor sensitivity
**Copper:** Works with zinc for thyroid regulation
**Vitamin A (Retinol):** Enhances thyroid hormone receptor function

<Info>
**Food first, always.** These nutrients are better absorbed from whole foods than supplements.
</Info>

---

## Selenium (Critical for T3 Conversion)

### Why It Matters

**Selenium is essential for:**
- Converting T4 (inactive) to T3 (active thyroid hormone)
- Protecting thyroid from oxidative damage
- Immune function

**Deficiency signs:** Low temperature, fatigue, brain fog, hair loss

---

### Best Food Sources

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Brazil Nuts (Best Source)">
    **Selenium content:** 68-91 mcg per nut

    **How much:** 1-2 nuts daily = 100-180 mcg (perfect daily dose)

    **Why best:** Most bioavailable, easiest, cheapest

    **How to eat:**
    - Snack on 2 nuts daily
    - Chop and add to yogurt
    - Blend into smoothies

    **Storage:** Keep in fridge (high fat content can go rancid)

    <Warning>
    **Don't overdo it!** 3+ nuts daily can provide too much selenium (>400 mcg). Stick to 1-2 nuts.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Seafood & Fish">
    **High-selenium seafood:**
    - **Yellowfin tuna:** 92 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Sardines:** 45 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Halibut:** 47 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Shrimp:** 42 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Salmon:** 40 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Oysters:** 77 mcg per 3 oz (85g)

    **How much:** 2-3 servings per week

    **Benefits:** Also provides protein, iodine, and healthy fats
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Meat & Eggs">
    **Selenium from land animals:**
    - **Beef liver:** 28 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Beef (ground):** 16 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Chicken breast:** 22 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Eggs:** 15 mcg per egg
    - **Turkey:** 25 mcg per 3 oz (85g)

    **How much:** Daily protein sources (stack with Brazil nuts for optimal intake)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Daily target:** 100-200 mcg selenium

**Easiest way:** 2 Brazil nuts daily + regular protein sources

## Iodine (Thyroid Hormone Building Block)

### Why It Matters

**Iodine is essential for:**
- Producing T4 and T3 (thyroid hormones are made from iodine + tyrosine)
- Metabolic rate
- Energy production

**Deficiency signs:** Goiter, low metabolism, weight gain, brain fog

<Warning>
**Most people get enough iodine from iodized salt.** Deficiency is rare in the US unless you avoid salt or only use sea salt/Himalayan salt (which don't contain iodine).
</Warning>

---

### Best Food Sources

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Iodized Salt (Easiest)">
    **Iodine content:** ~45 mcg per 1/4 tsp (1g)

    **How much:** Season your food liberally with iodized salt

    **Why:** Easiest, cheapest way to get iodine

    **Brands:** Any table salt labeled "iodized"

    **Note:** Sea salt, Himalayan pink salt, and kosher salt do NOT contain iodine
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Seafood & Seaweed">
    **High-iodine sources:**
    - **Seaweed (kombu):** 2,000+ mcg per sheet (use sparingly!)
    - **Cod:** 99 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Shrimp:** 35 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Tuna:** 17 mcg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Eggs:** 24 mcg per egg

    **How much:** 2-3 servings seafood per week

    <Warning>
    **Seaweed warning:** Kombu and other seaweeds can contain 1000s of micrograms of iodine - way too much. Use occasionally for flavor, not daily.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Dairy & Eggs">
    **Iodine from animal products:**
    - **Yogurt:** 75 mcg per cup
    - **Milk:** 56 mcg per cup
    - **Eggs:** 24 mcg per egg
    - **Cheese:** 12 mcg per slice

    **How much:** Daily dairy consumption covers most needs
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Daily target:** 150-300 mcg iodine

**Easiest way:** Use iodized salt on your food + eat dairy/eggs regularly

## Zinc (Thyroid Hormone Production)

### Why It Matters

**Zinc is essential for:**
- Thyroid hormone production
- Receptor sensitivity (helps cells respond to thyroid hormone)
- Immune function
- Wound healing

**Deficiency signs:** Low thyroid function, poor immunity, slow healing, hair loss

---

### Best Food Sources

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Shellfish (Best Source)">
    **High-zinc shellfish:**
    - **Oysters:** 74 mg per 3 oz (85g) (off the charts!)
    - **Crab:** 6.5 mg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Lobster:** 3.4 mg per 3 oz (85g)

    **How much:** Oysters once per week = entire week's zinc

    **Note:** Oysters are the highest zinc food on earth
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Red Meat">
    **Zinc from meat:**
    - **Beef:** 7 mg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Lamb:** 5 mg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Pork chops:** 2.9 mg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Ground beef:** 5.3 mg per 3 oz (85g)

    **How much:** Daily protein sources provide solid zinc

    **Benefits:** Also provides protein, iron, B12
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Dairy & Eggs">
    **Zinc from dairy:**
    - **Cheese (cheddar):** 3 mg per oz (28g)
    - **Milk:** 1 mg per cup
    - **Yogurt:** 1.3 mg per cup
    - **Eggs:** 0.6 mg per egg

    **How much:** Daily dairy adds up over time
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Seeds & Nuts (Lower Bioavailability)">
    **Plant sources:**
    - **Pumpkin seeds:** 2.2 mg per oz (28g)
    - **Cashews:** 1.6 mg per oz (28g)
    - **Almonds:** 0.9 mg per oz (28g)

    **Note:** Plant zinc is less bioavailable (phytates block absorption)

    **Better:** Get zinc from animal sources
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Daily target:** 8-11 mg zinc

**Easiest way:** Eat red meat daily + dairy

## Copper (Works with Zinc)

### Why It Matters

**Copper is essential for:**
- Balancing zinc (too much zinc depletes copper)
- Thyroid hormone metabolism
- Iron absorption
- Energy production

**Deficiency signs:** Anemia, fatigue, poor immunity, thyroid issues

<Note>
**Copper and zinc must be balanced.** High zinc intake (>30 mg daily) can cause copper deficiency. Get both from food.
</Note>

---

### Best Food Sources

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Organ Meats (Best Source)">
    **High-copper organ meats:**
    - **Beef liver:** 12 mg per 3 oz (85g) (off the charts!)
    - **Chicken liver:** 0.4 mg per 3 oz (85g)

    **How much:** Liver once per week = entire week's copper

    **How to eat:**
    - Pan-fry with butter and onions
    - Make pâté
    - Add to ground beef (hide the taste)
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Shellfish">
    **Copper from seafood:**
    - **Oysters:** 7.6 mg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Crab:** 1 mg per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Lobster:** 1.6 mg per 3 oz (85g)

    **How much:** Oysters once per week covers copper needs

    **Bonus:** Also high in zinc (balanced!)
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Dark Chocolate">
    **Copper from cacao:**
    - **Dark chocolate (70-85%):** 0.5 mg per oz (28g)

    **How much:** 1-2 oz (28-56g) dark chocolate daily = good copper source

    **Brands:** Look for high-cacao percentage (70%+)
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Nuts & Seeds">
    **Copper from plants:**
    - **Cashews:** 0.6 mg per oz (28g)
    - **Sunflower seeds:** 0.5 mg per oz (28g)
    - **Almonds:** 0.3 mg per oz (28g)

    **Note:** Moderate amounts okay (but avoid high PUFA nuts)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Daily target:** 0.9-1.3 mg copper

**Easiest way:** Liver once per week + dark chocolate daily

## Vitamin A (Retinol - Thyroid Receptor Function)

### Why It Matters

**Vitamin A (retinol) is essential for:**
- Thyroid hormone receptor sensitivity
- Vision, immunity, skin health
- Gene expression

**Important:** True vitamin A is **retinol** (from animal foods), not beta-carotene (from plants). Many people convert beta-carotene poorly.

**Deficiency signs:** Night blindness, dry skin, poor immunity, thyroid issues

---

### Best Food Sources

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Liver (Best Source)">
    **Vitamin A from liver:**
    - **Beef liver:** 16,898 IU per 3 oz (85g) (insane amount!)
    - **Chicken liver:** 5,340 IU per 3 oz (85g)

    **How much:** Liver once per week = entire week's vitamin A

    **Why best:** Most bioavailable, highest concentration

    **How to eat:**
    - Pan-fry with butter and onions
    - Make pâté
    - Freeze into small portions, swallow like pills (if you hate the taste)
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Dairy & Eggs">
    **Vitamin A from dairy:**
    - **Butter (grass-fed):** 350 IU per tablespoon
    - **Egg yolks:** 245 IU per egg
    - **Whole milk:** 395 IU per cup
    - **Cheese:** 284 IU per oz (28g)

    **How much:** Daily dairy + eggs provide good amounts

    **Note:** Grass-fed dairy has way more vitamin A than conventional
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Fish">
    **Vitamin A from seafood:**
    - **Cod liver oil:** 4,080 IU per tsp (but it's a supplement)
    - **Salmon:** 149 IU per 3 oz (85g)
    - **Mackerel:** 214 IU per 3 oz (85g)

    **How much:** 2-3 servings seafood per week helps
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Daily target:** 3,000-10,000 IU retinol

**Easiest way:** Liver once per week + butter, eggs, and dairy daily

## Supplements vs. Food

<Warning>
**Food is always better than supplements** for these nutrients. Here's why:

**Selenium:** Brazil nuts are more bioavailable than supplements
**Iodine:** Easy to get from iodized salt + dairy
**Zinc:** Better absorbed from meat than supplements
**Copper:** Liver once per week = entire week's needs
**Vitamin A:** Retinol from liver/butter is way better than beta-carotene supplements
</Warning>

**When to supplement:**
- If you can't eat Brazil nuts (selenium supplement)
- If you avoid salt and dairy (iodine supplement)
- If you don't eat meat (zinc supplement - but really, eat meat)
- If you hate liver (vitamin A supplement or cod liver oil)

**See:** [Supplement Cheat Sheet](/course/bonus/supplement-cheat-sheet) for specific supplement recommendations

## Foods to AVOID for Thyroid Health

### Goitrogens (If You're Sensitive)

**What they are:** Compounds that can interfere with thyroid function (in large amounts)

**Foods:**
- Raw cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower, kale, cabbage)
- Soy (tofu, soy milk, edamame)

**Note:** Cooking destroys most goitrogens. Cooked broccoli is fine. Raw kale smoothies daily might be problematic.

**Do you need to avoid them?** Probably not unless you're eating massive amounts raw. Most people are fine with cooked cruciferous veggies.

---

### PUFAs (Biggest Thyroid Suppressant)

**The real enemy:** Seed oils (canola, soybean, sunflower, safflower, corn, grapeseed)

**Why they suppress thyroid:**
- Damage mitochondria (where energy is made)
- Interfere with thyroid hormone transport
- Cause inflammation
- Suppress T4 → T3 conversion

**Action:** Eliminate completely (see [Safe Foods Shopping List](/course/bonus/safe-foods-shopping))

## The Bottom Line

<Note>
**You don't need exotic superfoods for thyroid health.**

**The essentials:**
- 2 Brazil nuts daily (selenium)
- Red meat daily (zinc, selenium)
- Butter with meals (vitamin A)
- Eggs and dairy (iodine, vitamin A, zinc)
- Iodized salt on food
- Liver once per week (vitamin A, copper)

**Cost:** Less than $10/week extra

**Effectiveness:** Way better than supplements

**Eliminate PUFAs completely** - that's more important than any food you add.
</Note>
